Our Story

Founded in Faith,
Built for Purpose

St Gabriel Catholic Primary School was established in 2008 by the Archangel Gabriel Catholic Church Missionaries — responding to a call to bring quality, values-centred education to the families of Gatundu and the wider Kiambu County highlands.

The school opened as a modest day school running Classes 1 to 10 under Kenya's 8-4-4 curriculum. Guided by the missionaries' vision, it grew steadily in enrolment, infrastructure, and reputation — eventually transitioning into a fully accredited CBC day and boarding school offering Grade 1 through Grade 9 under Kenya's 2-6-3-3-3 framework.

Set among the rolling tea and coffee farms of Gatundu, our campus blends modern learning facilities with deep spiritual roots — producing graduates who are academically capable, morally grounded, and practically equipped for the challenges of a changing Kenya.

Education

Academic Excellence
Through CBC

We implement Kenya's Competency-Based Curriculum fully — building learners who think critically, create confidently, and engage meaningfully with the world around them.

CBC Curriculum

Learner-centred and project-based. Subjects span Languages, Mathematics, Science and Technology, Creative Arts, Social Studies, Agriculture, and CRE across all nine grades.

Computer Lab

Our ICT lab holds 30 desktop computers with reliable internet. Digital literacy is taught from Grade 4 — preparing learners for a technology-driven Kenya and economy.

Science Laboratory

A fully stocked science lab supports CBC Integrated Science and Science and Technology practicals from Grade 4 upward — making experiments a core part of learning, not a luxury.

Creative Arts Studio

Drawing, painting, textile design, and crafts are taught by specialist teachers in a dedicated studio. CBC recognises creative talent as equal to academic — and we live by that.

Agriculture Practicals

Our farm is a live classroom. Learners grow crops, raise animals, and study soil science — earning marks for hands-on CBC Agriculture practicals rather than textbook theory alone.

Parental Engagement

Termly parent-teacher forums, open days, and CBC project showcases ensure every family is an active partner in their child's learning journey from Grade 1 to Grade 9.

Co-Curricular Life

Activities & Enrichment

Beyond the classroom, St Gabriel learners build confidence, teamwork, and character through sport, music, drama, and culture.

Football Programme

Football is the heartbeat of co-curricular life at St Gabriel. We run separate boys and girls teams from Grade 4 upward, competing in Kiambu County inter-primary tournaments organised by KPSSA. Training is held every Tuesday and Thursday afternoon under our qualified coach on our maintained grass pitch.

  • Gatundu Zone Champions — Girls Under-12, 2024
  • Kiambu County Quarter-finalists — Boys, 2023 KPSSA Tournament
  • Annual inter-house football competition — Term 1 every year

Athletics & Track

Our programme covers sprints, relays, long jump, high jump, shot put, and cross-country. St Gabriel athletes consistently represent the school at zonal, county, and national level. The annual Sports Day in Term 2 draws parents and the wider Gatundu community.

  • Three learners selected for national athletics — 2023
  • Cross-country team — Kiambu County Top 5, 2024
  • Annual Sports Day — Term 2, full community attendance every year

Drama Club

Our Drama Club produces original plays in both English and Kiswahili — exploring themes of identity, community, faith, and Kenyan culture. The club competes in the Kenya Schools and Colleges Drama Festival annually. Rehearsals run every Wednesday and Friday in the school hall.

  • Kiambu County Drama Festival — Best Group Play, 2023
  • Annual Christmas nativity production at Archangel Gabriel Parish
  • Easter passion play performed at the parish church every Lent

Music Programme

Music runs through every aspect of school life at St Gabriel — from classroom songs to the Mass choir. Our programme covers vocal training, keyboard, percussion, and traditional Kenyan instruments. We compete in the Kenya Music Festival every year and our choir has qualified to regional level for three consecutive years.

  • Central Region Music Festival Finalists — Primary Choir, 2024
  • School choir leads all three weekly masses
  • End-of-year music concert open to all parents — December each year

Choral Verse

Choral verse is one of St Gabriel's proudest traditions. Groups of learners perform poetry with rhythm, expression, and movement — building language skills, teamwork, and stage presence simultaneously. Our team has become a formidable competitor at zone and county level under our English and Literature teachers.

  • Zone Champions — Choral Verse, 2022, 2023 and 2024
  • Kiambu County Runners-Up — Choral Verse, 2024
  • Annual school poetry slam open to all grades every Term 2

Visual Art, Drawing & Design

Our art studio is stocked with paints, clay, fabric, and recycled materials. Learners produce drawings, paintings, textile designs, and sculptures that fill the school with colour and creativity. A major Art Exhibition is held every December — parents and community members are invited to view and purchase learner work.

  • Annual Art Exhibition — December, all grades displayed

Student Life

Clubs & Societies

St Gabriel learners are active members of thriving clubs that build leadership, discipline, service, and community — beyond the timetable.

Scouts Troop

Our Kenya Scouts Association troop meets every Friday afternoon. Learners develop leadership, outdoor survival skills, and community service values — parading proudly at school assemblies and national events.

St John Ambulance

Our St John unit trains learners in first aid, emergency response, and health awareness. Members wear their uniform with pride and are first responders during school events, sports days, and community gatherings.

The debate club builds critical thinking, public speaking, and confidence. Learners compete in inter-school debates across Kiambu County in both English and Kiswahili, developing the communication skills CBC demands.

St Gabriel takes environmental stewardship seriously — a core Catholic social teaching. The club leads tree planting, waste management, and conservation projects on campus and in the wider Gatundu community.

Residential Life

Home Away
From Home

Boarders follow a structured daily routine from 5:30 AM wake-up through to 9:30 PM lights out — with two hours of teacher-supervised evening prep every weekday to maximise academic progress.

  • On-campus nurse and 24-hour first aid room
  • Parental visiting day — first Saturday of every month
  • Holiday transport arranged for all boarding learners
Nutrition

Meals & Nutrition

A well-fed learner is an attentive learner. Our trained cooks prepare wholesome Kenyan meals every day — using fresh produce from our own school farm.

6:45 AM — Breakfast

Morning Tea & Uji

Boarding learners begin the day with hot porridge (uji wa wimbi or uji wa mahindi), two mandazi, and sweet milky tea — the classic Kenyan school breakfast that fuels a full morning of learning.

10:00 AM — Break

10 O'Clock Break

All learners — day and boarding — receive a mid-morning snack during the 20-minute break. Githeri (maize and beans), a bread roll, or a banana is served with a cup of tea to keep energy levels steady through to lunch.

12:30 PM — Lunch

Full Kenyan Lunch

Lunch rotates daily: rice with beef stew and sukuma wiki, ugali with fried fish and cabbage, pilau with kachumbari, or chapati with a bean curry. Vegetables are grown on the school farm for maximum freshness.

6:30 PM — Supper

Boarders Supper

Evening supper for boarding learners includes a warm, filling meal: ugali with beef stew, or rice with fried chicken on Fridays. A cup of warm mala (fermented milk) or Milo completes the meal before evening prep begins.

Practical Agriculture

Our School Farm

St Gabriel's three-acre farm is one of our proudest assets. Learners from Grade 4 participate in structured agricultural activities as graded CBC practicals — growing food, raising animals, and learning the science and economics of farming firsthand.

The farm grows maize, kale (sukuma wiki), tomatoes, beans, and other crops suited to the Gatundu highlands. All produce goes directly to our school kitchen — ensuring fresh, nutritious meals for every learner every single day.

Our animals serve both educational and economic roles. Learners study animal husbandry, basic veterinary care, and small-scale farming economics as live CBC Agriculture practicals — not just textbook exercises.

Learner Support

Health & Counselling

A learner who feels well and safe can learn. Our on-campus dispensary and counselling services ensure every child is supported — physically and emotionally.

School Dispensary

Our on-campus dispensary is staffed by a qualified nurse, available from 7:00 AM to 9:00 PM every school day. It handles minor illnesses, first aid, and medication management for boarding learners. Serious cases are referred to Gatundu Level 5 Hospital, just 4 km away.

Counselling Room

Our trained counsellor provides a safe, confidential space for learners navigating academic pressure, peer relationships, family challenges, or personal wellbeing. Individual and group sessions are available. Every child has the right to be heard — and at St Gabriel, they are.

Getting Here

School Transport

Our reliable bus fleet serves Gatundu town and surrounding areas — ensuring every learner arrives safely and on time, every school day.

Daily Routes

Buses cover Gatundu town, Karuri, Kiganjo, Komothai, and surrounding sub-locations. Morning pick-up from 6:00 AM. Afternoon drop-off follows immediately after school dismissal at 3:30 PM.

Safety Standards

All vehicles hold current Ministry of Education certification and are serviced every term. Vetted drivers, first aid kits on every bus, and an assigned supervising teacher for junior grade routes.

Trips & Excursions

Our fleet supports annual educational trips to Nairobi, Amboseli, Tsavo East, and the Kenya coast — connecting CBC learning to real-world experiences no textbook can replicate.

Beyond the Classroom

Educational Trips

Annual excursions connect CBC learning to the real world of Kenya's parks, cities, coast, and cultural heritage — experiences that stay with learners for a lifetime.

Grade 6 to 9

Wildlife — Tsavo East

Learners visit Tsavo East National Park, connecting Science, Geography, and Environmental Education with live wildlife encounters. They observe the Big Five, study ecosystems, and engage with KWS rangers on conservation.

Grade 5 to 8

Nairobi City Experience

The Nairobi trip includes the Kenya National Museum, Parliament Buildings, City Hall, and Nairobi National Park — bringing Social Studies, Civic Education, and History to life through direct discovery in the capital.

Grade 7 to 9

Coastal Kenya — Mombasa

The annual coastal trip visits Fort Jesus, Haller Park, the Old Town, and Diani Beach — engaging learners with Kenyan history, marine ecosystems, and cultural diversity in one of the country's most vivid settings.
